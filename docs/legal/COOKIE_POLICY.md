## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website or use a web application. They are used to make services work correctly, remember your preferences, and provide data to site operators about how the service is used.

**Similar technologies we may use:**

- **Local Storage / Session Storage** — browser-based storage used to persist data across page loads or within a single session
- **IndexedDB** — browser database for offline or complex client-side data
- **Pixels and web beacons** — small invisible tracking elements that record whether a page or email was loaded
- **Device fingerprinting** — technical signals used exclusively for fraud detection and security purposes

## 2. Cookie Categories

We classify cookies into four categories based on their purpose:

### 2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the Service to function. They enable core functions such as authentication, security, and session management. They cannot be disabled. No consent is required for their placement under the ePrivacy Directive.

| Cookie / Technology | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Authentication token (JWT)** | Keeps you securely logged in to your account | Session / up to 30 days |
| **Session cookie** | Maintains your active session state on the server | Session |
| **CSRF token** | Prevents cross-site request forgery attacks | Session |
| **2FA state token** | Tracks two-factor authentication step completion | Session |
| **Locale preference (`app_locale`)** | Stores your selected interface language | 1 year |
| **Stripe fraud prevention** | Essential anti-fraud signals required during payment checkout (set by Stripe.js) | Session / up to 1 year |

### 2.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance the functionality and personalization of the Service by remembering your choices. They are not strictly necessary but improve your experience.

| Cookie / Technology | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **User preferences** | Stores dashboard layout, generation settings, and UI preferences | 1 year |
| **Onboarding state** | Tracks completion of onboarding steps so you are not shown them repeatedly | 1 year |
| **Firebase auth persistence** | Maintains authentication state for users who sign in with Google | Persistent (until sign-out or revocation) |
| **Checkout state** | Remembers your checkout progress within the current session | Session |
| **Referral attribution** | Tracks inbound referral source for credit attribution | 30 days |

### 2.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how users interact with the Service, which pages are visited, and where technical errors occur. Data collected is aggregated and pseudonymized. They are placed only with your consent.

| Cookie / Technology | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal product analytics** | Tracks page views, feature usage, generation events, and user flows for our own analysis | Up to 2 years |
| **Error and performance tracking** | Captures JavaScript errors and performance metrics for debugging and reliability improvements | Session |
| **A/B testing flags** | Records which version of a feature or interface you are assigned to during experiments | 30 days |

### 2.4 Third-Party Cookies

Certain features of the Service rely on embedded third-party services that may set their own cookies on your device. We do not control these cookies. They are governed by the third party's own privacy and cookie policies.

| Third Party | Purpose | Their Privacy Policy |
|---|---|---|
| **Google / Firebase** | Authentication (Google Sign-In), session state management | [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy) |
| **Stripe** | Payment processing, fraud detection via Stripe.js and Stripe Radar | [Stripe Privacy Policy](https://stripe.com/privacy) |

## 6. Your Consent and Cookie Controls

### 6.1 Consent

When you first visit the Service, we present a cookie consent notice. Strictly necessary cookies are placed automatically. Functional, analytics, and third-party cookies are placed only after you provide consent.

You may withdraw consent or change your preferences at any time — withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing that occurred before withdrawal.

### 6.2 Browser-Level Controls

Most browsers allow you to control cookie behavior via settings. You can:
- View and delete individual cookies
- Block all third-party cookies
- Clear cookies on browser close
- Block cookies from specific websites

| Browser | Settings location |
|---|---|
| **Chrome** | Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data |
| **Firefox** | Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data |
| **Safari** | Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data |
| **Microsoft Edge** | Settings → Cookies and site permissions |
| **Opera** | Settings → Advanced → Privacy & security → Cookies |

### 6.3 Effect of Disabling Cookies

Disabling **strictly necessary** cookies will prevent you from logging in and using the Service. Disabling functional or analytics cookies will not prevent access to core features but may reduce personalization and our ability to identify and fix technical problems.
